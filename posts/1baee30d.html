一些有关 TSM & GDPR 的问题与回答

一些有关TSM和GDPR的问题

1(a) Telecoms Single Market (TSM)

The Telecommunications Single Market first mentioned as a regulation adopted by the European Union in 2015. And it aims to provide an “open Internet” by requiring telecom operators to treat all Internet traffic equally[1]. In another word, the Telecoms Single Market means no discrimination across borders[2].
The Digital Single Market is a strategy proposed by the European Commission in 2015 which intend to remove virtual borders, boost digital connectivity, and make it easier for consumers to access cross-border online content[3]. TSM is the foundation of this strategy.

1(b) GDPR and TSM relationship

Both TSM and GDPR are the basis for the implementation of the DSM strategy[4].
TSM is about regulating telecom operators to make the digital information transport easier between different areas, while the GDPR protects the rights of consumers (natural persons) by regulating the organizations’ behaviors.
For their purposes, the two are the same. For their results, both have transformed the market environment better.

2(a) Limited for its purpose

Organizations should only collect necessary personal data for a specific purpose [5]. And they must clearly state what that purpose is. If the organization want to use personal data for the other purposes, it will need to get the permission from the user separately[6].

2(b) Necessary and compatible

As said above, the organizations should only collect data for as long as necessary to complete their shown purpose.
Furthermore, when the organizations or staff dealing with a certain process, they should be given only the necessary data to achieve its processing purposes[6]. Meanwhile, these data must be enough related with the processor and the processing purpose.

2(c) Storage limitation

Organizations need to delete personal data when it is no longer necessary. Alternatively, the personal data should be anonymized or modified until it no longer relates to individual[5].
The definition of the period of ‘no longer necessary’ above will vary between industries and the reasons that data is collected. This period is usually acceptable if it is logical and reasonable. Meanwhile, the organizations should record and show these reasons properly[6].
However, this period should also refer to the record[7] that established by IGA in 2016. This gives the organizations a reference standard.

2(d) Integrity and confidentiality

Organizations should arrange security measures to protect the personal data from unauthorized or unlawful processing and accidental loss, destruction or damage.
Furthermore, more attention should be paid to special categories of data, which require a greater level of protection due to their sensitive nature[6].

3(a) Privacy by Design

Privacy by design is a systems engineering approach that aims to ensure protection for the privacy of individuals. It considerate the privacy issues integrally from the very beginning of the development of products, services, business practices, and physical infrastructures[8].
This approach can provide a systematic overview of the personal privacy so that the organizations can avoid many security problems and reduce many risks at the beginning, while the users can control their personal data more efficiently and comprehensively.

3(b) Pseudonymization

The pseudonym is an identifier associated with an individual. Pseudonymization technology is a kind of privacy engineering technology that allows users to use pseudonyms to switch between using personal data and using anonymous data.
Pseudonymization reduces the risk of being directly identified by personal data and exposing sensitive data to unauthorized people and employees[9].
The pseudonymization technique is reversible compared with another privacy technology, that recommended by GDPR, anonymization, which will irreversibly alter the data, meaning that users can also access the data through the “keys” after the pseudonymization. At the same time, this feature also makes regulation much more easier than the anonymization[10].

4(a) Exemptions from PDPA

As a response of the GDPR, the Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA) actually supplemented the original regulations.
However, it generally does not apply to[11]:

  1. Individuals acting in either a domestic or personal capacity.
  2. Public agencies.
  3. Employees performing actions in the context of their employment with a particular business or organization.
  4. Government agencies.
  5. Any other organization or category if personal data that may be prescribed.

4(b) DPO and PDPC

A Data Protection Officer (DPO) plays an important role in company or organization. They must ensure that the company’s policy meets with PDPA guidelines. They have responsibility of turning data protection into a competitive advantage for the company as well, which would lead to building trust in the wider data ecosystem[12][13].
Personal Data Protection Commission (PDPC) provides a baseline standard of protection, PDPA, for personal data in Singapore. It includes various requirements governing the collection, use, disclosure and care of personal data in Singapore[14].
In a word, PDPC provides the basic requirement and standard of the organizations’ policy and then it plays a role of ruler and punishes the organizations that violate the regulations. While the DPO whom plays an agent role in the organization will help build the specifical policy that suit with their own and then keep both policy and data healthy.

4(c) DNC registry for telemarketing

PDPC also provides for the establishment of a national Do Not Call (DNC) Registry[15].
Individuals can register their phone number on the DNC Registry website first. All the registered phone numbers will be collected and reserved safely. When some organizations want to send messages or other forms of promotion, they must compare the target phone number with the registered list that provided by DNC Registry. If that Phone number is on the list, the message will be forbidden to send.
The DNC Registry provisions came into force on 2 January 2014 and the main data protection rules on 2 July. From then on, when the organization trying telemarketing, they are forced to firstly check the target user whether willing to receive the promotion or not[16].
From the perspective of the PDPC, DNC Registry is an important part of the realization of PDPA, and it promotes each other with PDPA.
DNC takes the needs of consumers into consideration, which is a significant manifestation of people’s right to personal private data. It also prevents organizations from over-promoting without considering to user privacy.
